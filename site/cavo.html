The scale-up argument

From 20 to 32 keys within the existing footprint

Revenue efficiency

Adding 12 keys to Phase 1's 20 increases total revenue by 57%. The plot accommodates this without footprint expansion — the infrastructure is already sized for the full build-out.

OPEX leverage

OPEX grows by only 20% when scaling from 20 to 32 keys. Shared management, a single F&B operation, and fixed infrastructure costs spread across more units — the economics improve non-linearly with scale.

Exit positioning

A 32-key stabilised hotel at €1,050,000 annual EBITDA on the Ionian coast commands a significantly higher exit multiple than a 20-key asset. Scenario C is designed for investors whose primary orientation is the 5-year exit, not current yield.
