The concept

A hotel and a restaurant under one roof

Most boutique hotels depend on room revenue alone. This asset combines short-term accommodation with a ground-floor gastro-hub that serves both hotel guests and the local Ioannina community — two independent income streams from a single 350 m² building.

The upper floors hold 5 boutique suites with automated check-in, designed for year-round short-term rental. The ground floor operates as a gastro-hub: 141 m² of indoor restaurant space with a 105 m² outdoor terrace. Each income stream reinforces the other, while each can operate independently.

The asset is ready-to-operate as a concept. Full documentation — financial model, floor plans, legal structure, and operator shortlist — is available on request. Privately owned, off-market.

The space

350 m² across four components

The building is organised across three above-ground levels and a production basement. Upper floors are dedicated to the five suites. The ground floor hosts the full gastro-hub operation — indoor dining, a glazed terrace, and direct access to the street. The basement supports the kitchen and storage without reducing lettable space.

Suites range from 21 to 33 m² with automated guest access systems. The format is optimised for operational efficiency: minimal staff overhead, high-margin F&B, and year-round demand from Ioannina's academic and business population.

Why Ioannina

Four structural advantages of this market

A growing IT cluster with no premium hotels

TeamViewer, Deloitte, and PwC have established operations in Ioannina. Business demand for quality accommodation significantly outpaces supply. The city is becoming Greece's secondary technology hub — the infrastructure for premium hospitality has not kept up.

Year-round city, not a seasonal resort

University, medical centre, and administrative traffic keeps both occupancy and F&B revenue stable across twelve months. There are no seasonal shutdowns. This is the structural advantage a coastal resort cannot replicate.

15–20% asset appreciation expected

Regional infrastructure expansion — new road connections and airport upgrades — is projected to drive capital growth within a 3-year horizon. Entry at this stage captures appreciation that is not yet priced into the market.

Golden Visa eligible

The asset price and structure comply with Greek Golden Visa residency requirements. EU residency pathway available at this investment level. Ioannina falls within the standard €250,000 threshold zone, unlike Athens and the major island destinations.
