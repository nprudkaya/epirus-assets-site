Overview

Mytikas Seafront Estate

11,326 m² on a natural cape, 8 metres above the Ionian Sea. The plot sits on a natural plateau that creates an amphitheatre effect — every guestroom in either development scenario has unobstructed sunset and sea views. The elevation provides natural privacy and protects the asset from coastal erosion.

This is one of the last undeveloped plots of this scale with direct seafront access on the Mytikas coastline. The surrounding area is substantially built out. Scarcity at this quality level drives both the long-term exit value and the competitive positioning of any hotel developed on the site.

Two development scenarios are modelled: a 42-key premium combination targeting maximum ADR and brand positioning, and a 52-key maximum capacity scenario targeting yield. Both are analysed to full EBITDA and ROI. Full documentation — land title, planning capacity, financial model, and development brief — is available on request.

Why this asset

Four reasons to enter Preveza at this stage

01

One of the last large seafront plots available

The Mytikas coastline is almost fully developed. This 11,326 m² plot with direct sea access and an 8-metre elevation is a finite asset. Scarcity of this type does not correct — it compounds, and it drives long-term exit value independently of hotel performance.

02

Preveza as a premium destination

Increasing demand from high-net-worth European travellers is positioning Preveza as a serious alternative to overcrowded Greek islands. The region has supply constraints that the islands do not — and entry prices that reflect where the market is today, not where it will be in five years.

03

Infrastructure investment accelerating access

New highway links between Preveza, Ioannina, and Patras and the expansion of PVK Aktion International Airport are increasing connectivity and driving tourist arrivals year-on-year. The infrastructure build-out is underway — not planned.

04

17.5% ROI — above comparable Mediterranean assets

Entering at land acquisition stage allows you to capture the developer's margin. At 17.5% ROI under Scenario B, this asset significantly outperforms comparable Mediterranean hotel investments — without the premium pricing of island or Athenian markets.

The land

A natural cape, 8 metres above the Ionian Sea

Amphitheatre topography

The plot's natural plateau elevation creates an amphitheatre effect across the entire site. Every room in both development scenarios faces unobstructed sea and sunset views — not just the premium suites. This is not a design decision; it is a geographic given.

Coastal resilience

The 8-metre elevation above sea level provides natural protection from coastal erosion and wave action. This is a long-term infrastructure advantage — it reduces maintenance costs, protects the investment against climate-related coastal risk, and meets international hotel brand standards for coastal resilience.

Development capacity

2,265 m² above-ground GFA permitted on the 11,326 m² plot. Sufficient for either development scenario with room for amenity infrastructure — pools, landscaping, and hospitality support buildings within the planning envelope.
