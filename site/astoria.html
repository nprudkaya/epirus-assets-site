Overview

Urban Suites & Secret Garden rooftop

A deep renovation project converting an existing building at Ioannina's most strategic commercial intersection into a 30-key smart-luxury boutique hotel. The project is off-market and ready for modernisation.

The investment case rests on four compounding factors: a location that guarantees consistent foot traffic, an operational model that reduces staffing costs through automation, a floor plan that increases inventory by 42% within the existing building envelope, and a rooftop F&B concept with no direct competitor in the local market.

The total investment basis of €1,750,000 includes acquisition costs, the full renovation budget, and all closing and legal fees. The projected annual net operating income is €331,975, with a target return on equity of 19.1%.

What makes this asset

Four compounding advantages

01

Profit geometry

Engineering a 42% increase in inventory — from 21 to 30 rooms — within the existing building envelope of approximately 1,100 m². Revenue per square metre rises without expanding the footprint or triggering a new permitting process.

02

Triple-A location

Situated at Ioannina's most strategic commercial intersection — high visibility, constant foot traffic from both business and leisure travellers, and proximity to the city's corporate district where demand for premium accommodation is structural, not seasonal.

03

Smart-luxury model

Full integration of IoT systems — contactless check-in, smart climate control, and centralised management software — enables high-margin operations with significantly reduced staffing. The model is designed to run lean from day one.

04

The Secret Garden

A 160 m² rooftop bar concept designed as an urban oasis. There is no direct competitor in the Ioannina F&B market at this format. The Secret Garden functions as both a revenue stream and a positioning asset — it is what guests remember and return for.

Development strategy

How the transformation works

Reconfiguration

Transformation of existing floor plates from standard 5-room layouts to 7-unit high-efficiency clusters. The change is structural but contained within the building envelope — no external expansion is required.

Modernisation

A down-to-the-studs renovation including all new MEP systems (mechanical, electrical, plumbing) and premium interior finishes. The result is a new asset within an existing shell — carrying the locational advantages of an established building without its operational liabilities.

Technology integration

Contactless check-in, smart climate control, and centralised management software are specified from the planning stage, not retrofitted. This ensures the lean operational structure is built in rather than bolted on.
