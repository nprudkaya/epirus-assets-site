In 2026, the Greece Golden Visa remains one of Europe’s most attractive residency-by-investment programs for non-EU citizens seeking security, mobility, and long-term asset growth.

The program offers a 5-year renewable residence permit through qualifying investments in Greek real estate, with access to the Schengen Zone and the opportunity to include family members in a single application. Depending on the location and type of property, the minimum investment starts from €250,000 and may reach €400,000–€800,000 in high-demand areas such as Athens, Thessaloniki, Mykonos, and Santorini.

Today’s market creates several important opportunities for investors:

✔ Strategic entry into the European market
✔ Property ownership in a growing Mediterranean economy
✔ Potential long-term capital appreciation
✔ Stable rental income through long-term leasing
✔ Lifestyle benefits including healthcare, education, climate, and safety
✔ No permanent residence requirement to maintain the permit

For many international investors, the Greek Golden Visa is no longer just a residency solution — it is part of a broader wealth preservation and diversification strategy.

At the same time, recent regulatory updates have introduced stricter rules and important limitations that investors must understand before making a decision.

Current restrictions include:

• Different investment thresholds depending on the region
• Minimum property size requirements in many areas
• Ban on short-term rental use (Airbnb-style rentals) for Golden Visa properties
• Increased due diligence and proof-of-funds verification
• Citizenship is not automatic and requires long-term physical residence in Greece

These changes make professional guidance more important than ever. Choosing the wrong property can reduce profitability, delay approval, or create legal complications during the application process.

A successful Golden Visa investment today requires more than simply buying real estate. It requires selecting the right location, understanding future market demand, calculating realistic returns, and ensuring full legal compliance from the beginning.
